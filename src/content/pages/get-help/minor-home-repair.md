**NOTE: The Minor Home Repair Program is not accepting applications at this time.**

HFHCL works to assist low-income homeowners, including veterans, seniors, and disabled with minor exterior home repairs with a primary focus on accessibility.

## Examples of Minor Home Repairs

1. Fence repair or replacement
2. Yard debris removal and clean-up
3. Pathway repair or replacement
4. Entry step repair or replacement
5. Porch repair or replacement
6. Exterior painting
7. Deck repair or replacement

Habitat does not paint homes where lead-based paint is present nor do we paint second stories. Because projects are cost limited, it usually means only one item in the examples list can be completed.

## Do I qualify?

The exterior areas of the home must be in disrepair or unsafe, and HFHCL's scope of work must significantly benefit the homeowner. The homeowner must be unable to physically and/or financially complete the work themselves.

The household gross income, including household members 18 years and over, must fall below 80% Area Median Income (AMI) based on the current year of H.U.D. Income Guidelines.

The homeowner is required to pay a portion of the material costs using a sliding scale and payment plan. The payment plan begins the month following completion of the project and is paid through equal monthly installments up to two years with 0% interest.

The project must be economically feasible under the Program and adhere to the scope of repairs HFHCL offers. The total cost of the project must fall between $500 and $2,500, with the exception to accessibility ramps which has a cap of $7,500.

The home must be located within HFHCL's service area. The homeowner must be current on property taxes, mortgage payments if applicable, and homeowner's insurance premiums. The home must be an owner-occupied, single-family house or mobile home, and the primary residence of the owner.

## Required documents

To certify that applying homeowners are in the Area Median Income and qualified to apply, HFHCL requires homeowners to include the following documents in their application:

- Completed application, signed by all applicant adults
- Copies of two months of pay stubs for all working adults in the home, if applicable
- Documentation of all other income for two months, such as social security, SSD, TANF, child support, or unemployment
- Copy of the most recent federal tax statement for each adult, any form 1040 version, including W2 forms if applicable
- Proof of citizenship or permanent legal residency for each adult, such as a copy of birth certificate, valid US passport, voter's registration, or legal permanent residence card
- Copy of driver's license or Oregon ID for each adult
- Copy of Mortgage Statement
- Copy of most recent Property Tax Statement
- Copy of Homeowner's Insurance Declarations Page
- Copy of military discharge paperwork, DD Form 214, if applicable

## Process

Before a home is selected for the Minor Home Repair Program, the home is evaluated. A home visit must occur in order to determine the scope of work and determine the estimated cost of the project.

After the application, home visit, and scope of work are approved, Habitat Central Lane meets with the homeowner to review any potential repayment plans and sign a partnership agreement.

Upon completion, the Director of Construction calculates the actual costs of the Minor Home Repair. The homeowner reviews and signs a Certificate of Completion and receives a payment plan. Habitat Central Lane asks the homeowner to participate in a short survey about how the repairs were beneficial.
