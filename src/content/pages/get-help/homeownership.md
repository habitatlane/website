**Please note that this program is not accepting new applications.**

Habitat for Humanity of Central Lane helps low-to-moderate-income families achieve affordable homeownership by building homes, which are then sold with affordable mortgages. Applicants must demonstrate a need for housing, willingness to partner, including 200-250 hours of participation, and ability to repay the mortgage. Mortgages are set at 30% of homeowners gross income. The income is based on the income at the time of closing.

Takeaways:

- We are not an emergency housing agency
- All homeowners pay a mortgage, 30% of their gross income
- We must follow Federal Fair Housing Act

## Do I qualify?

- A household's gross income before taxes must be between 40% to 80% of the Area Median Income (AMI) for our geographical area, based on HUD guidelines and adjusted annually.
- Current housing must be insufficient due to overcrowding, temporariness, defective and unsafe conditions, or other reasons.
- Applicants may be paying significantly more than 30% of their gross income for rent.
- Applicants must have lived or worked, on-site work not remote, in the affiliate service area for the previous 12 months. A remote worker must show proof that they lived in the HFHCL service area while working the remote position.
- Applicants must be citizens or permanent residents.

## Application process

The process begins with a Pre-Application that opens when Habitat Central Lane has homes in process.

**Currently the Pre-Application process is closed.**

When applications are open, Habitat Central Lane posts dates and deadlines on the website, in the eNewsletter via email, on social media, and in the ReStore in the form of posters and handouts. The first phase is filling out a prequalification form. This determines if gross income falls within the Habitat Income Guidelines of 40%-80% AMI.

If an applicant qualifies, they move to the next phase and receive a letter with a list of all required documentation and the date it is due. For security reasons, Habitat Central Lane does not accept email of applications; mail or delivery to the Habitat office is acceptable. The information is then reviewed by the Home Buyer Selection Committee to determine if the applicant is eligible to continue the process toward becoming a Habitat homeowner.

If initial eligibility requirements are met, such as not owning a home in the past three years and having lived or worked onsite in the affiliate service area for the previous 12 months, an informational interview is scheduled to evaluate the household's preparedness for homeownership.

If approved by Habitat for Humanity of Central Lane's Board of Directors, homeowners begin the Homeownership Program requirements. The Homeowner Support Committee guides them through the process, including homebuyer education classes and completing a minimum of 200-250 participation hours. Participation hours can be earned on construction sites, at special events, in the ReStore, or in the office.
